CREATION PROCESS

Step 1

We started off by discussing our individual goals and interest in order to help us decide on the theme and objective of our AI project. After reaching a consensus, we decided to create an AI that could generate reviews and synopsis.

Step 2

From there, we started looking at various databases in search of clean and filtered data. Using Kaggle, we collectively assembled datasets on anime, tv shows, and movie synopsis/reviews.

Step 3

After preparing our data, we converted text into vectors using Word2Vec for the AI to understand. Then we moved to chopping up our text and cleaning it up with the help of NLTK.

Step 4

Next up we started training multiple AIs to generate synopsis and positive/negative comments with the help of aitextgen.

Step 5

Our final task was to deploy our product to the web with Flask


Why a synopsis and review generator?

We see our project as an idea creation/brainstorming tool that can be really helpful and fun at the same time! We wanted something that could inspire and create random ideas for writers and filmakers. Our product is also a fun outlet that generates fun and silly stories!"

Dataset information

For this product, we utilized various datasets taken from Kaggle, the sizes of which ranged from 5,000 all the way up to 85,000 synopses and comments. Some notable datasets include movie reviews from IMDB, anime synopses from MyAnimeList, and TV show reviews from Metacritic.

Ai training models

Before the AI could be fed information, our team made sure to preprocess the data by getting rid of ellipses, extra tags, and implementing to_csv functions. Training the AI was a difficult and time-consuming process. In fact, sometimes it would take up to 6 hours to train a single model. Additionally, rather than combining all the databases, we decided to train 9 separate AIs.

Overcoming difficulties

While training the models, we noticed that we kept running out of RAM. In order to fix this issue, we had to edit the source code so that the AI would make the generation length shorter. We also ran into the issue of figuring out sentiment analysis for generating positive and negative comments. Our members solved this problem by splitting the dataset of comments into positive and negative ones based on rating systems.
